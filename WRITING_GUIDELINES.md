# LiveSales Blog Writing Guidelines
**Last Updated: 2026-02-12**

## Core Philosophy: Value Over Volume

> "Users have 2 minutes. Make them count."

### The Truth About Blogs
- Users don't want to "explore your content" - they want **answers to specific problems**
- Every second wasted on fluff = losing trust
- AI-generated spam doesn't build businesses - **authentic expertise does**
- Conversion happens when you solve problems, not when you "create awareness"

### Our Commitment
1. **Radical honesty** - We talk about our weaknesses AND competitors' strengths
2. **Hyper-utility** - Every blog must solve a real business problem
3. **No distractions** - No share buttons, no comment sections, no newsletter popups during reading
4. **Interactivity as value** - Calculators that actually help make decisions, not vanity metrics

## Competitive Landscape (Be Honest About It)

### Real Competitors
1. **Allegro Analytics** (native, free/cheap)
   - **Their Strength**: Built-in, no integration, free basic tier
   - **Their Weakness**: Data obfuscation (point scale instead of numbers), limited to Allegro only
   - **Our Angle**: Multi-marketplace + transparency

2. **DataHawk** (advanced analytics platform)
   - **Their Strength**: Superior data accuracy, great visualizations, 2x daily crawls
   - **Their Weakness**: Expensive, complex for small sellers, primarily Amazon-focused
   - **Our Angle**: Accessible for Polish market, Allegro expertise

3. **Helium 10** (full-suite Amazon tool)
   - **Their Strength**: AI insights, highest accuracy (71%), comprehensive features
   - **Their Weakness**: Amazon-centric, overwhelming for beginners, US-market focus
   - **Our Angle**: Polish market, multi-marketplace simplicity

4. **Google Analytics** (universal web analytics)
   - **Their Strength**: Free, universally known, good for customer journey
   - **Their Weakness**: NOT marketplace-specific, requires custom setup, no profitability calculations
   - **Our Angle**: Built for marketplace sellers, profitability-first

### What Competitors Don't Cover (Our White Space)
1. **True cost of returns** - reverse logistics costs (2/3 of product value!), processing labor, lost value
2. **Multi-marketplace profitability** - currency normalization, regional margin variance (27% Germany vs 19% US)
3. **Cash flow vs. profit timing** - 30-day payment delays, seasonal stress, payout frequency differences
4. **Honest comparison of tools** - including when competitors are better choices
5. **Real operational costs** - tax complexity, subscription fatigue, hidden fees
6. **Strategic portfolio allocation** - when to exit vs. scale on each marketplace

## Blog Architecture: Hub & Spoke Model

### Content Cluster Strategy

**Goal**: Build topical authority with interconnected content where main "hub" blogs drive traffic, satellite blogs provide depth.

#### Hub Blogs (4-6 Core Topics)
These are comprehensive, high-traffic posts that convert visitors into users:

1. **"Complete Guide to Marketplace Profitability"** (Master hub)
   - Links to all other content
   - 5000+ words, exhaustive
   - Target: "jak zmierzyć rentowność"

2. **"Allegro vs. Multi-Marketplace Strategy: The Data-Driven Decision"**
   - Honest comparison
   - When to stay Allegro-only vs. expand
   - Target: "rozszerzenie sprzedaży poza Allegro"

3. **"The Real Cost of Returns: Why 20% Return Rates Kill Your Margins"**
   - Deep dive on hidden costs
   - Calculator: Return Impact Calculator
   - Target: "koszty zwrotów e-commerce"

4. **"Cash Flow vs. Profit: Why Your 'Profitable' Business Can Still Fail"**
   - 30-day payment cycles
   - Seasonal stress
   - Calculator: Cash Flow Timing Calculator
   - Target: "problemy z płynnością finansową e-commerce"

#### Satellite Blogs (15-20 Supporting Articles)
These support hub blogs and target long-tail keywords:

**Around Hub 1 (Profitability)**:
- "How to Calculate Allegro Fees: The Complete Breakdown"
- "Minimum Viable Margin for Marketplace Sellers: 20% or 30%?"
- "Category-Level Profitability Analysis: Finding Your Winners"
- "Allegro Ads ROI: When to Scale, When to Cut"

**Around Hub 2 (Multi-Marketplace)**:
- "Allegro Analytics vs. Third-Party Tools: Honest Comparison"
- "When Google Analytics Fails Marketplace Sellers"
- "DataHawk vs. Helium 10 vs. LiveSales: Feature Comparison"
- "2025 Allegro Market Shifts: Competition from Temu and Erli"

**Around Hub 3 (Returns)**:
- "Reverse Logistics Costs: The 66% Rule"
- "How to Price Products Accounting for Return Rates"

**Around Hub 4 (Cash Flow)**:
- "Understanding Marketplace Payout Cycles: Allegro vs. Amazon vs. eBay"
- "Seasonal Cash Flow Planning for E-commerce"

### Internal Linking Rules
1. **Every satellite links to its hub** (contextually, not in boilerplate)
2. **Hubs link to related satellites** (2-5 links per hub)
3. **All blogs within 3 clicks of homepage**
4. **Use descriptive anchor text** (not "click here")
5. **2-5 internal links per post maximum** (quality over quantity)

## Universal Blog Structure (The Template)

### Pre-Writing Checklist
- [ ] Target keyword identified
- [ ] User intent understood (what problem are they solving?)
- [ ] Competitor content reviewed (what's missing?)
- [ ] Data/stats collected (original research or credible sources)
- [ ] Calculator/interactive element planned (if applicable)
- [ ] Related internal links identified

### Mandatory Sections

#### 1. H1: Clear, Specific Question or Promise
**Bad**: "Allegro Profitability Guide"
**Good**: "Jak zwiększyć rentowność na Allegro: 7 strategii opartych na danych"

**Rules**:
- One H1 per post
- Include target keyword naturally
- Promise specific value (number, outcome, timeframe)
- 50-70 characters for SEO

#### 2. TL;DR (Top or Bottom - Pick One)
**Format**: 80-100 words OR 3-5 bullet points

**Top TL;DR** (for action-oriented posts):
```markdown
## TL;DR - Kluczowe wnioski

• Minimum 20-30% marża brutto to próg rentowności po wszystkich kosztach
• Koszty zwrotów to 2/3 wartości produktu (nie tylko shipping!)
• Allegro Analytics ukrywa dane liczbowe - użyj narzędzi external dla pełnej transparentności
• Kalkulator poniżej pokazuje Twoją realną marżę po ALL kosztach
• 68% sprzedawców multi-marketplace brakuje zunifikowanej analityki
```

**Bottom TL;DR** (for informational deep-dives):
Place after conclusion as executive summary.

**Impact**: 35% surge in AI visibility, reduced bounce rate

#### 3. Opening Paragraph (Front-Load the Answer)
**Rule**: Answer the main question in first 2 sentences.

**Bad**:
> "Marketplace profitability is a complex topic that many sellers struggle with. In this comprehensive guide, we'll explore various factors..."

**Good**:
> "Realną rentowność marketplace obliczasz odejmując od przychodu: COGS + prowizje platformy (8-15%) + koszty wysyłki + advertising + zwroty (typowo 2/3 wartości produktu). Minimum 20-30% marża brutto to próg rentowności - poniżej tego tracisz pieniądze nawet jeśli 'sprzedajesz'."

**Why**: AI systems and impatient users need immediate answers.

#### 4. Content Body (Logical H2 → H3 Hierarchy)

**Structure Example**:
```markdown
## Zrozumienie kluczowych metryk rentowności

### Czym jest marża brutto?
[40-60 word concise answer suitable for featured snippet]
[Supporting details, examples, data]

### Czym jest zysk netto?
[40-60 word concise answer]
[Supporting details]

## Krok po kroku: Analiza rentowności

### Krok 1: Oblicz przychód
[Specific instructions]

### Krok 2: Odejmij wszystkie koszty
[Cost categories breakdown]
```

**Rules**:
- Never skip heading levels (H1 → H2 → H3, not H1 → H3)
- Each H2/H3 should clearly describe what readers will learn
- Avoid vague labels like "Introduction" or "More Info"
- Target 40-60 words for answers under each H3 (featured snippet optimization)

#### 5. Interactive Element (Calculator or Visualization)

**When to Include**:
- Every hub blog MUST have one
- Satellite blogs: optional but encouraged

**Types**:
1. **Calculators**:
   - Margin Calculator (input: price, fees, costs → output: net margin, %)
   - Return Impact Calculator (input: return rate, product value → output: real cost)
   - Cash Flow Timing Calculator (input: sales, payout cycle → output: cash timeline)
   - Multi-Marketplace ROI Comparator

2. **Data Visualizations**:
   - Cost breakdown charts (Recharts)
   - Profitability comparison tables
   - Timeline/trend graphs

**Requirements**:
- Must be genuinely useful (not vanity metrics)
- Allow file upload where relevant (e.g., CSV with product costs)
- Export results (PDF + Excel)
- Save to localStorage (user convenience)
- Mobile-responsive
- Clear input labels with examples
- Explain methodology (show the math)
- Include data visualization (chart showing cost breakdown)

#### 6. Data & Statistics (Credibility)

**Rule**: Include 3-5 specific data points with sources.

**Good Examples**:
- "Research shows reverse logistics costs average 2/3 of product value (Billgosling, 2024)"
- "Multi-marketplace sellers grow 2.3x faster, but 68% lack unified analytics (NovaData, 2025)"
- "Allegro margin variance: 27% in Germany vs. 19% in US (Allegro Analytics, 2024)"

**Impact**: 30-40% more AI citations with credible data

#### 7. Honest Competitive Comparison

**When to Include**: Whenever discussing solutions or strategies

**Format - Comparison Table**:
```markdown
| Tool | Strengths | Weaknesses | Best For |
|------|-----------|------------|----------|
| Allegro Analytics | Built-in, free basic, no setup | Data obfuscation, Allegro-only | Single-marketplace Polish sellers |
| DataHawk | Best accuracy, great visualizations | Expensive, complex | Power users, large catalogs |
| Google Analytics | Free, universal | Not marketplace-specific | DTC stores, not marketplace sellers |
| LiveSales | Multi-marketplace, transparent data, Allegro expertise | [Be honest about our limitations] | Growing Polish marketplace sellers |
```

**Tone**:
- Acknowledge competitor strengths sincerely
- Be transparent about our weaknesses
- Help readers choose the RIGHT tool (even if it's not us)
- Users trust honesty more than hype

#### 8. Related Internal Links (Contextual)

**Rules**:
- 2-5 links per post maximum
- Place in body content, not boilerplate
- Use descriptive anchor text with keywords
- Link to hub if it's a satellite, or to satellites if it's a hub

**Example**:
> "Jeśli chcesz głębiej zrozumieć [jak optymalizować koszty Allegro Ads dla lepszej rentowności](#link), sprawdź nasz szczegółowy przewodnik."

#### 9. Conclusion with Next Step

**Format**:
```markdown
## Podsumowanie

[2-3 sentence recap of main points]

**Następny krok**: [Specific, actionable recommendation]
- Użyj kalkulatora powyżej, żeby obliczyć swoją realną marżę
- Przeanalizuj kategorię po kategorii za pomocą [narzędzia LiveSales](#)
- Przeczytaj [powiązany artykuł o cash flow](#) jeśli masz problem z płynnością
```

**No Generic CTAs**: Don't say "Contact us!" or "Try our product!" - give genuine value.

```json
    "@type": "Organization",

      "@type": "ImageObject",
      "url": "https://livesales.pl/logo.png"
    }
  },
  "description": "Brief description matching meta description",
  "articleBody": "Full article content..."

```

**Why**: Pages with complete schema are **3.7x more likely to be cited by AI tools**.

#### Featured Snippet Optimization
- Target long-tail question keywords
- Provide 40-60 word concise answers immediately after H2/H3 question headings
- Use lists, tables, and step-by-step formats
- Include FAQ schema for Q&A sections

#### Image SEO
- Feature image: 1200x675px (optimal for Google Discover)
- Alt text: descriptive, includes keyword naturally
- File name: descriptive (e.g., `allegro-margin-calculator.jpg`)
- Compress for fast loading

#### Meta Tags
- **Title**: 50-70 characters, include target keyword
- **Description**: 150-160 characters, compelling, includes keyword
- **OpenGraph**: For social sharing (even without share buttons, AI tools scrape this)

### Content SEO

#### E-E-A-T Signals (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trust)

**Experience** (Most Important in 2026):
- Share real marketplace examples from our data
- Include case studies with specific numbers
- Show original testing/research
- Use "Our Experience" or "What We Found" sections
- Include original screenshots, graphs, analysis

**Expertise**:
- Go beyond obvious answers
- Use technical terms correctly with explanations
- Provide comprehensive, deep content
- Show mastery through specificity

**Authoritativeness**:
- Cite credible external sources (studies, industry reports)
- Link to authoritative sites (government data, research institutions)
- Get featured on industry lists and articles
- Build relationships for backlinks

**Trust**:
- Transparent methodology (how we got data, what we tested)
- Clear author information with credentials
- Accurate, verifiable information
- Professional presentation
- Honest about limitations

#### Keyword Optimization
- **Primary keyword** in:
  - H1 (once)
  - First paragraph (once)
  - URL slug
  - Meta title and description
  - 1-2 H2 headings (naturally)
  - Image alt text

- **Related keywords** throughout body (LSI keywords):
  - "rentowność sprzedaży" → "marża brutto," "zysk netto," "ROI"
  - "Allegro analytics" → "analityka marketplace," "śledzenie sprzedaży"

- **Keyword density**: 1-2% (natural, not stuffed)

#### For ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity Citations

**Key Requirements**:
1. **Front-load answers** - First 2 sentences must answer the question
2. **Include data & statistics** - 30-40% more citations with specific numbers
3. **Create self-contained semantic blocks** - Each section should stand alone
4. **Use clear hierarchy** - H2/H3 structure helps AI parse content
5. **Add schema markup** - 3.7x more likely to be cited

**Example of AI-Friendly Block**:
```markdown

Poniżej tego poziomu, po odjęciu prowizji platformy (8-12%), kosztów wysyłki (5-10%), reklamacji (2-5%), i zwrotów (średnio 66% wartości produktu na zwrócony item), zysk netto spada poniżej zera. [Źródło: Analiza 500+ sprzedawców Allegro, LiveSales 2025]
```

**Why This Works**:
- Answers question in first sentence
- Includes specific percentages
- Cites source
- Logically complete (doesn't require context)

## Writing Tone & Style

### Voice Principles
1. **Direct and practical** - No corporate jargon, no fluff
2. **Honest to a fault** - Acknowledge what we don't know, what competitors do better
3. **Empathetic** - We understand seller pain points because we've been there
4. **Data-driven** - Claims backed by numbers, not vague assertions
5. **Polish-first** - Write natively in Polish, don't translate from English

### What to Avoid
❌ **Hype and superlatives**: "The BEST tool ever!" "Revolutionary analytics!"
❌ **Vague claims**: "Increase profits significantly" (say "20-30% margin improvement")
❌ **Corporate speak**: "Leverage synergies to optimize KPIs"
❌ **Clickbait**: "You Won't Believe This One Trick..."
❌ **Empty phrases**: "In today's competitive marketplace..." (get to the point)

### What to Embrace
✅ **Specificity**: "2/3 of product value in return costs" not "high return costs"
✅ **Transparency**: "We're not best for X, but great for Y"
✅ **Practicality**: "Here's exactly how to calculate..." not "Consider analyzing..."
✅ **Humanity**: "We tested this with 50 sellers and here's what happened..."

## Calculator Design Guidelines

### Philosophy
Calculators are the **core differentiator** - make them hyperuseful, not marketing gimmicks.

### Features Checklist
- [ ] Genuinely solves a calculation problem (not just "see how great we are")
- [ ] Accept file uploads where relevant (CSV, Excel with product data)
- [ ] Export results (PDF report + Excel file)
- [ ] Save to localStorage (users can return to saved calculations)
- [ ] Mobile-responsive (works on phone)
- [ ] Clear input labels with examples
- [ ] Explain methodology (show the math)
- [ ] Include data visualization (chart showing cost breakdown)

### Calculator Types to Build

1. **Marketplace Margin Calculator**
   - Inputs: Product price, COGS, marketplace fees %, shipping cost, return rate %, ad spend
   - Outputs: Gross margin, net margin, breakeven price, profit per unit
   - Visualization: Pie chart showing cost breakdown

2. **Return Impact Calculator**
   - Inputs: Product price, return rate %, reverse logistics cost, refurbishment cost, lost value %
   - Outputs: Real cost per return, monthly return cost projection, margin impact
   - Visualization: Bar chart comparing "assumed" vs. "real" return costs

3. **Cash Flow Timing Calculator**
   - Inputs: Monthly sales, marketplace payout cycle (days), seasonal variation
   - Outputs: Cash flow timeline, minimum working capital needed, danger periods
   - Visualization: Timeline graph showing cash inflows/outflows

4. **Multi-Marketplace ROI Comparator**
   - Inputs: Sales by marketplace, fees by marketplace, currencies
   - Outputs: Normalized profitability comparison, best/worst performers, reallocation recommendation
   - Visualization: Comparative bar chart across marketplaces

5. **Allegro Ads ROI Calculator**
   - Inputs: Ad spend, attributed sales, margin %
   - Outputs: ROAS, net profit impact, break-even ROAS, recommendation (scale/cut)
   - Visualization: Trend line showing profitability threshold

### Technical Stack for Calculators

**Forms & State**:
- React Hook Form (for complex multi-field calculators)
- Zustand (for multi-step calculators with shared state)
- React useState (for simple 3-5 field calculators)

**Validation**:
- Zod (already in project) + @hookform/resolvers

**Charts**:
- Recharts (recommended - 100kb, responsive, great with Tailwind)

**Export**:
- PDF: jsPDF + html2canvas
- Excel: xlsx (SheetJS)

**Storage**:
- localStorage (built-in, no dependencies)

**Styling**:
- Tailwind utilities matching blog design
- `bg-gradient-brand` for CTA buttons
- `border-gray-200` for cards
- `rounded-xl` for consistency

### Embedding in Blog Posts

**Method 1: MDX Components** (Recommended for new posts)
1. Change file extension from `.md` to `.mdx`
2. Import calculator component in frontmatter
3. Insert with `<MarginCalculator client:visible />`

**Method 2: Component Islands** (For existing `.md` files)
1. Conditionally render in `[slug].astro` template
2. Check slug and insert component

## Examples of Great vs. Bad Content

### Example 1: Opening Paragraph

❌ **Bad** (Vague, Slow):
> "In the competitive world of e-commerce, understanding profitability is crucial for success. Many sellers struggle with this complex topic. In this comprehensive guide, we will explore the various factors that contribute to marketplace profitability, including fees, shipping costs, and more. By the end of this article, you'll have a better understanding of how to improve your margins."

✅ **Good** (Direct, Specific):
> "Realną rentowność marketplace obliczasz odejmując od przychodu: COGS + prowizje platformy (8-15%) + koszty wysyłki + advertising + zwroty (typowo 2/3 wartości produktu). Minimum 20-30% marża brutto to próg rentowności - poniżej tego tracisz pieniądze nawet jeśli 'sprzedajesz'. Ten przewodnik pokazuje dokładnie, jak obliczyć każdy element za pomocą interaktywnego kalkulatora poniżej."

**Why the good version wins**:
- Answers question immediately
- Includes specific numbers (8-15%, 20-30%, 2/3)
- Front-loads the most critical information
- Mentions the value prop (interactive calculator)

### Example 2: Competitive Comparison

❌ **Bad** (Hype, Defensive):
> "While other tools like DataHawk and Helium 10 claim to offer analytics, they fall short in many areas. LiveSales provides superior insights and is the best choice for Polish marketplace sellers. Our advanced features outperform the competition."

✅ **Good** (Honest, Helpful):
> "DataHawk oferuje najlepszą dokładność danych (aktualizacja 2x dziennie) i świetne wizualizacje - jeśli masz duży katalog i budżet $300+/miesiąc, to excellent wybór. Helium 10 ma najszerszy zestaw funkcji i AI insights, ale skupia się głównie na Amazon i rynku US. Allegro Analytics jest built-in i darmowe w podstawowej wersji, ale obfuskuje dane liczbowe (pokazuje 'punkty' zamiast liczb) i działa tylko na Allegro. LiveSales jest najlepszy jeśli: (1) sprzedajesz na Allegro i rozszerzasz się multi-marketplace, (2) potrzebujesz transparentnych danych bez ukrywania liczb, (3) chcesz zrozumieć realną rentowność a nie tylko wolumen sprzedaży."

**Why the good version wins**:
- Acknowledges specific competitor strengths (DataHawk accuracy, Helium 10 features)
- Identifies the right use case for each tool
- Honest about Allegro Analytics limitation (data obfuscation)
- Positions LiveSales clearly without exaggeration
- Helps user choose the RIGHT tool (even if not us)

### Example 3: Data Usage

❌ **Bad** (Vague):
> "Returns can significantly impact your profitability. Many sellers underestimate the cost of processing returns, which can hurt your bottom line. It's important to account for these expenses in your calculations."

✅ **Good** (Specific, Cited):
> "Koszty reverse logistics średnio wynoszą 66% wartości zwróconego produktu - nie tylko shipping, ale też processing labor, lost value, i refurbishment (Billgosling Research, 2024). Przy 20% stopie zwrotów, produkt sprzedany za 100 PLN z 30 PLN marżą brutto faktycznie generuje tylko 16.80 PLN zysku netto po uwzględnieniu kosztów zwrotów (20% * 100 PLN * 66% = 13.20 PLN strat). Większość sprzedawców o tym nie wie, bo platformy nie pokazują tych kosztów explicitly."

**Why the good version wins**:
- Specific number: 66% (not "significantly")
- Cited source (Billgosling Research, 2024)
- Concrete example with math (100 PLN → 16.80 PLN)
- Explains WHY sellers miss this (platforms don't show it)
- 30-40% more likely to be cited by AI

## Final Reminders

### The Golden Rules
1. **Value > Volume**: One great post beats 10 mediocre posts
2. **Honesty > Hype**: Acknowledge competitors' strengths and our weaknesses
3. **Utility > Awareness**: Solve problems, don't just "create content"
4. **Data > Opinions**: Back claims with specific numbers and sources
5. **Users > SEO**: Write for humans first, optimize for machines second (but do both!)

### When in Doubt
- Would you read this if you found it on Google?
- Does this solve a real problem you've personally experienced?
- Is every sentence necessary, or is there fluff?
- Have you cited sources for claims?
- Would you honestly recommend a competitor if they're better for the user's specific case?

### The 2-Minute Test
Imagine a stressed seller with 2 minutes to spare:
- Can they find the answer they need?
- Is it buried in fluff or front-loaded?
- Can they take action immediately (calculator, clear next step)?

**If the answer to any is "no," rewrite.**
